# Ayushman Bharat — Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PM-JAY)

## What is This Scheme?

Ayushman Bharat — Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PM-JAY) is the biggest health insurance scheme by the Government of India. It was launched on 23 September 2018. Under this scheme, poor and vulnerable families get **free treatment up to Rs 5 lakh (five lakh rupees) per family per year**. The card is also called the "Ayushman Card" or "Golden Card."

The biggest benefit is that when you are admitted to a hospital, **you do not have to pay anything from your own pocket**. The government pays for everything. This includes expenses for 3 days before hospital admission and 15 days after discharge (medicines, follow-up tests, etc.).

## Who is Eligible — Who Can Get This Card?

This scheme is for families that are listed in the **Socio-Economic Caste Census 2011 (SECC 2011)** data. The main eligible groups are:

### In Rural Areas:
- Families living in houses with kutcha walls and kutcha roof
- Families with no male member aged 16 to 59 years
- Families headed by a woman with no adult male earning member
- Scheduled Caste (SC) and Scheduled Tribe (ST) families
- Landless families who earn money through manual daily labour
- Families with a disabled member and no able-bodied adult
- Homeless families
- Families involved in manual scavenging
- Primitive tribal groups
- Legally released bonded labourers

### In Urban Areas:
- Rag pickers, beggars, domestic workers
- Street vendors, cobblers, hawkers
- Construction workers, plumbers, painters, welders
- Sweepers, gardeners, tailors
- Rickshaw pullers, auto drivers, shop workers
- Washermen, security guards, coolies

**Note:** Many families with a BPL (Below Poverty Line) ration card may also be eligible. However, eligibility is based on the SECC 2011 data. Some states have also added their own additional lists.

## How Much is Covered?

- **Up to Rs 5 lakh per family per year** — completely free treatment
- **All family members are covered** — husband, wife, children, elderly parents
- **No limit on family size** — whether your family has 5 members or 10
- **Pre-existing diseases are also covered** — if you already have a disease, it will still be treated
- Cover includes: hospital room, doctor fees, medicines, diagnostic tests, surgery, ICU charges, and food during hospital stay

## What Treatments and Diseases are Covered?

The scheme covers approximately **1,929 types of treatments**, including:

- **Heart disease:** Bypass surgery, angioplasty, heart valve replacement
- **Cancer:** Chemotherapy, radiotherapy, cancer surgery
- **Kidney problems:** Dialysis, kidney transplant
- **Eye problems:** Cataract surgery, retina surgery
- **Bone and joint problems:** Knee replacement, hip replacement, fracture surgery
- **Childbirth:** Normal delivery, cesarean (C-section) delivery
- **Children's diseases:** Pneumonia, diarrhea, birth defects
- **Brain and spine:** Brain surgery, spine surgery
- **General surgery:** Appendix, hernia, gallbladder operation

## How to Get the Ayushman Card — Easy Steps

### Step 1: Check if You Are Eligible
- **On the website:** Go to https://pmjay.gov.in, click on "Am I Eligible," enter your mobile number and ration card number
- **By phone:** Call **14555** (toll-free) and tell them your name and ration card number
- **At CSC centre:** Visit your nearest Common Service Centre (Jan Seva Kendra) and ask them to check

### Step 2: Gather Required Documents
- Aadhaar card (of all family members)
- Ration card
- Mobile number (linked with Aadhaar)
- Passport-size photograph

### Step 3: Visit a CSC Centre or Hospital
- Go to your nearest **Common Service Centre (CSC)** / Jan Seva Kendra
- OR go to the Ayushman Bharat counter at any **government hospital**
- OR visit the Ayushman help desk at any **empanelled private hospital**

### Step 4: Complete eKYC (Verification)
- Your identity will be verified using your Aadhaar card
- Biometric verification (fingerprint/thumbprint) will be done
- Your photo will be taken

### Step 5: Receive Your Card
- The card is made instantly — it takes just a few minutes
- A digital card can also be sent to your mobile phone
- **There is NO fee to make this card** — if anyone asks for money, file a complaint

## Where Can You Get Treatment?

- Free treatment is available only at **empanelled (listed) hospitals**
- Both **government and private hospitals** are empanelled
- To find the nearest empanelled hospital:
  - Call **14555**
  - Visit https://pmjay.gov.in and click "Find Hospital"
  - Ask at your district Chief Medical Officer (CMO) office

## How to Get Treatment — What to Do at the Hospital

1. Go to an empanelled hospital
2. Find the **"Ayushman Mitra"** or help desk at the hospital
3. Show your **Ayushman Card and Aadhaar Card**
4. Complete biometric verification (fingerprint)
5. The hospital will start your treatment
6. **You do not have to pay anything** — if the hospital asks for money, file a complaint at 14555

## Helpline and Complaints

- **Ayushman Bharat Helpline:** **14555** (toll-free, available 24 hours, 7 days a week)
- **Complaints:** If you face problems making the card, if a hospital refuses treatment, or if a hospital demands payment — call 14555 to complain
- **Online complaint:** https://grievance.pmjay.gov.in
- **ASHA worker:** You can also ask your village ASHA didi for help with the process

## Important Things to Remember

- This card is **completely free** — no premium, no annual fee, no hidden charges
- The card **works anywhere in India** — you can get treatment at any empanelled hospital in any state (this is called portability)
- **Pre-existing diseases are covered from day one** — there is no waiting period
- **Rs 5 lakh cover renews every year** — you get a fresh Rs 5 lakh every year
- If your name is not in the SECC 2011 list but you are poor, ask about your state's additional schemes — many states have added their own beneficiary lists
- **Cashless and paperless** — the entire process is designed so that you do not need to pay anything or fill out complicated forms

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Does the card work in other states?**
A: Yes. You can use the Ayushman card at any empanelled hospital in India, even if it is in a different state.

**Q: What if I do not have an Aadhaar card?**
A: Aadhaar is the primary document for eKYC. If you do not have one, first get an Aadhaar card made at your nearest Aadhaar enrolment centre.

**Q: My family member is already sick. Can we still get the card?**
A: Yes. There is no waiting period. Even if someone is already ill, they can get the card and start treatment immediately.

**Q: Can I use this at a private hospital?**
A: Yes, but only at empanelled private hospitals. Not all private hospitals are included. Call 14555 to check which hospitals near you accept the Ayushman card.
