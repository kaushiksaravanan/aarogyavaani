# Maternal Health — A Simple Guide for Pregnant Women in Rural India

## Signs of Pregnancy

If you notice these signs, you may be pregnant:

- **Missed period** — your monthly bleeding (menstruation) has not come for one or more months
- **Feeling nauseous or vomiting** — especially in the morning ("morning sickness")
- **Feeling very tired** — more tiredness than usual
- **Breasts feeling sore or swollen**
- **Needing to urinate (pass urine) more often**
- **Food cravings or dislike of certain foods**

If you notice these signs, **go to your nearest health centre or sub-centre and get a pregnancy test done**. You can also buy a home pregnancy test kit from a medical shop. Your ASHA didi can also help you with the test.

**Important:** Confirm your pregnancy as early as possible so that you can start getting proper care from the beginning.

## Antenatal Checkups — Why Regular Visits Are Important

Antenatal checkups (ANC) are health checkups done during pregnancy. Every pregnant woman should have **at least 4 antenatal visits** to a health centre. The recommended schedule is:

| Visit | When | What is Checked |
|-------|------|-----------------|
| 1st visit | As soon as pregnancy is confirmed (within first 3 months) | Blood test, urine test, weight, blood pressure, general health check |
| 2nd visit | 4th to 6th month | Baby's growth, mother's weight and blood pressure, iron level check |
| 3rd visit | 7th to 8th month | Baby's position, mother's blood pressure, swelling in feet, urine sugar |
| 4th visit | 9th month | Baby's position for delivery, check for any complications, plan for delivery |

During these visits, the doctor or nurse will:
- Check your **blood pressure** (high BP during pregnancy can be dangerous)
- Check your **weight** (healthy weight gain is important)
- Test your **blood** for anaemia (low blood), blood group, HIV, hepatitis, and syphilis
- Test your **urine** for infection and sugar
- Check the **baby's heartbeat** and growth
- Give you **TT injection** (tetanus toxoid) to protect you and the baby
- Give you **iron and folic acid tablets** (IFA tablets)

**Do not skip any visit.** These checkups help find problems early, when they can still be treated easily.

## Nutrition During Pregnancy — What to Eat

A pregnant woman needs to eat more and eat better. Good food keeps the mother healthy and helps the baby grow strong.

### Important Nutrients:

- **Iron:** Prevents anaemia (weakness due to low blood). Eat green leafy vegetables (palak, methi, sarson ka saag), jaggery (gud), dates (khajoor), pomegranate (anaar), and eggs. **Take your iron-folic acid (IFA) tablet every day** as given by the health worker — this is very important.
- **Folic acid:** Helps the baby's brain and spine develop properly. Found in green vegetables, dal, and citrus fruits. The IFA tablet contains folic acid.
- **Calcium:** Makes the baby's bones and teeth strong. Drink milk, eat dahi (curd), paneer, and ragi (finger millet).
- **Protein:** Helps the baby grow. Eat dal, chana, rajma, soyabean, eggs, fish, and milk.
- **Vitamins:** Eat seasonal fruits — guava, orange, papaya, banana, apple.

### Diet Tips:

- Eat **one extra meal** per day compared to before pregnancy (4 meals instead of 3)
- Eat a **variety of foods** — dal, roti, sabzi, fruits, milk, eggs
- Drink **plenty of water** — at least 8-10 glasses a day
- Do not eat **raw or undercooked meat**
- Avoid **too much tea or coffee** — it reduces iron absorption
- Do not consume **alcohol or tobacco** (gutka, paan, bidi, cigarette) — these seriously harm the baby
- Take your **IFA tablet after a meal** with some water or orange juice (the vitamin C helps the body absorb iron)

## Danger Signs During Pregnancy — When to Go to Hospital Immediately

If you notice any of these signs, **go to the hospital right away**. Do not wait. Every minute matters:

- **Heavy vaginal bleeding** — any bleeding from the vagina during pregnancy is a warning sign
- **Severe headache that does not go away** — especially with blurred vision or seeing spots
- **High fever** (above 100.4°F / 38°C) — can be a sign of infection
- **Severe pain in the abdomen (stomach area)** — especially if it does not stop
- **Swelling of face, hands, or feet** — sudden swelling can be a sign of high BP (pre-eclampsia)
- **Fits or convulsions** (body shaking/seizures) — this is eclampsia and is very dangerous
- **Baby stops moving** — if you do not feel the baby move for many hours (after 5 months)
- **Water leaking from the vagina before 9 months** — this means the water bag has broken early
- **Foul-smelling discharge from the vagina**
- **Painful or burning urination** with fever

**Remember:** It is always better to go to the hospital and find out everything is fine, than to stay at home and risk a serious problem. Call 108 (ambulance) or 102 (pregnancy helpline) for emergency transport.

## Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY) — Government Help for Safe Delivery

The Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY) is a government scheme that encourages pregnant women to deliver their babies in a hospital instead of at home. Delivering in a hospital is safer for both the mother and baby.

### Cash Benefit:
- **Rural areas:** The mother gets **Rs 1,400** for delivering in a government hospital or accredited private hospital
- **Urban areas:** The mother gets **Rs 1,000** for institutional delivery
- This money is given directly to the mother's bank account after delivery

### Who is Eligible:
- All pregnant women delivering in government health facilities
- Especially women from BPL (Below Poverty Line) families, SC/ST families
- In some states, all women are eligible regardless of income

### How to Get the Benefit:
1. Register your pregnancy at the nearest health centre early
2. Get your ANC checkups done regularly
3. Deliver your baby at a government hospital or accredited facility
4. The ASHA worker will help you with the registration and paperwork
5. Money will be transferred to your bank account (make sure you have a bank account with Aadhaar linked)

### Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (JSSK):
Under this related scheme, pregnant women are entitled to **completely free** delivery at government hospitals. This includes:
- Free delivery (normal and cesarean)
- Free medicines and consumables
- Free diagnostic tests (blood tests, ultrasound)
- Free food during hospital stay
- Free transport from home to hospital and back
- Free transport for the newborn baby if referred to another hospital

## ASHA Worker — Your Health Helper

The ASHA (Accredited Social Health Activist) worker is a trained health volunteer from your own village. She is there to help you during pregnancy and after the baby is born.

### How ASHA Didi Helps:
- Helps you **register your pregnancy** at the health centre
- **Reminds you** about ANC checkup dates
- **Accompanies you** to the health centre for checkups and to the hospital for delivery
- Makes sure you take your **iron-folic acid tablets** and **TT injections**
- Gives you **health advice** about nutrition, rest, and danger signs
- Helps you get benefits under **Janani Suraksha Yojana**
- Visits you **after delivery** to check on you and the baby
- Helps with **newborn care** — breastfeeding, warmth, immunization

**If you do not know your ASHA worker, ask at your village panchayat office or your nearest sub-centre or Primary Health Centre (PHC).**

## After Delivery — Postnatal Care

- **Breastfeed your baby within 1 hour of birth** — the first milk (colostrum) is very important for the baby's health
- **Breastfeed exclusively for 6 months** — no water, no other milk, no food — only breast milk
- Get your **postnatal checkup** within 48 hours after delivery, and again at 7 days and 42 days
- Continue taking **iron-folic acid tablets for 6 months** after delivery
- Get your **baby vaccinated** as per the schedule — BCG, OPV, Hepatitis B at birth
- Watch for danger signs in yourself: heavy bleeding, fever, foul-smelling discharge
- Watch for danger signs in the baby: not feeding, high fever, yellow skin (jaundice), fast breathing

---

**Remember: A safe pregnancy needs early registration, regular checkups, good food, iron tablets, and hospital delivery. Your ASHA didi and the nearest health centre are there to help you. Do not hesitate to ask for help.**

**Emergency numbers: 108 (ambulance), 102 (pregnancy helpline), 104 (health information)**
